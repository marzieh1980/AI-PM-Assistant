Title: Incident Management and Resolution Process

Overview:
This document outlines the standard process for handling IT incidents.

Incident Definition:
An incident is any unplanned interruption or reduction in quality of an IT service.

Steps to Resolve an Incident:
1. Identify and log the incident in the ticketing system.
2. Categorize the incident (e.g., Hardware, Software, Network).
3. Assign priority based on impact and urgency.
4. Investigate and diagnose the issue.
5. Apply a workaround or permanent fix.
6. Test the solution.
7. Close the incident after user confirmation.

Priority Levels:
- P1 (Critical): Service completely down
- P2 (High): Major functionality impacted
- P3 (Medium): Minor issue
- P4 (Low): Request or minor inconvenience

Escalation Process:
- If unresolved within SLA, escalate to Level 2 support.
- Critical incidents must be escalated immediately.

Communication:
- Keep users informed at regular intervals.
- Provide resolution details upon closure.

Example:
If a user cannot access email, log the issue, check server status, reset credentials if needed, and confirm with the user.
